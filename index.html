03 / How I Work

01 /

Find the real problem first.

Not every brief is wrong — but enough are. Before anything takes shape, I make sure the team is solving the same problem: clearly defined, genuinely understood, and agreed on across PM, Engineering, and Design. A well-built wrong solution is harder to kill than a bad one.

02 /

Complexity lives in the connections.

The hard problems rarely live in one screen — they live in what depends on what. Before I touch any interface, I map the system: the dependencies, the edge cases, the things that break somewhere else when you fix something here. That's not caution. That's how you know which part of the problem is yours to solve.

03 /

Design for the person, not the flow.

A system can be logically correct and still fail — because people don't follow the system's logic, they follow their own. I stay close to real users long enough to understand where the friction actually lives, not where it's supposed to be. The workflow on paper and the workflow in practice are rarely the same.

04 /

Point the right way, even when you can't go all the way.

Delivery constraints are real. Not every part of a solution can ship at once, and pretending otherwise leads to decisions that feel complete but aren't. I work with Engineering to understand what's possible and when — and I sequence releases that point in the right direction, even when the full solution has to wait.

05 /

Use AI to compress the loop, not the thinking.

The thinking doesn't change — the cycle does. I use AI across research, synthesis, and design exploration to move faster from question to validated direction. And I'm building workflows that close the gap between design decisions and frontend delivery — because the handoff is one of the last genuinely slow parts of the process.

04 / About

Sofia, Bulgaria

I've spent 9 years designing for products where complexity is the baseline — AI infrastructure, enterprise SaaS, operational platforms built for engineers, ops teams, and compliance officers. Not tools for casual use. Products where a poorly designed flow has real consequences.

The through-line is the same across every context: find the right problem before solving any problem, stay close to actual users, and build toward the right direction incrementally — even when the full solution has to wait. At Omniva I introduced discovery practice where none existed, aligned design and engineering around a shared direction through evidence, and shipped a signal-driven operational hub that reduced workflow task time by 35%.

I work best where design has to earn its place — alongside engineering, business goals, and real delivery constraints. That's the environment I know, and the one I'm looking for.
